Data Leakage

Introduction to data leakage

Data leakage is the unauthorised disclosure of sensitive or confidential information to the public or to unauthorised individuals or organisations.

Reason for data leakage:

  • Security leakage: A security Leakage in a system or application could result in unauthorized access to data.
  • Internal blunders: Employees or insiders may accidentally send sensitive data to the wrong recipients or leak it in public.
  • Malicious behavior: A deliberate insider or external attacker may knowingly steal and leak data for some purpose.
  • Insecure storage and transmission: Unencrypted data storage and transmission may be vulnerable to interception and theft by attackers.
  • Social engineering attacks: Attackers may obtain sensitive data through deception and misleading means.

Hazards of data leakage:

Data leakage can result in the following hazards:

  • Personal Privacy Leakage: If personally identifiable information, financial data, or medical records are compromised, this could result in a violation of personal privacy.
  • Financial Loss: When trade secrets or customer data are leaked, companies can suffer financial losses and lose their competitive advantage.
  • Legal Risk: Data Leakage expose companies to the risk of legal liability, lawsuits, and large fines.
  • Trust Issues: Security leakage such as data leakage can damage a company's image, leading to a drop in public trust and eroding public confidence.

How to prevent data leakage:

  • Improving Safety: Put in place strong measures to protect your data, like restricting who can access it, using encryption, asking for user confirmation and so on.
  • Employee Training: Provide continuous learning opportunities for employees, teach them how to handle confidential information with care, and educate them on best practices for protecting sensitive information.
  • Legal Adherence: Abide by all relevant laws, rules, and industry guidelines to make sure data handling meets compliance norms.
  • Backup and Disaster Preparedness: Make it a routine to back up critical data and have an emergency response plan in place to mitigate data loss risks.
  • Regular Audits: Make time to routinely examine the effectiveness of your data protection strategies and remedy any weak points you find.
