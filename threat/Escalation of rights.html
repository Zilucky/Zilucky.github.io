Privilege lifting

Introduction to privilege lifting attacks

A privilege lifting attack is the process by which an attacker attempts to gain a higher level of authority or privilege than their current authority.

Reason for privilege lifting attack:

  • Vulnerability Exploitation: An attacker may exploit a vulnerability in a system or application to gain privilege lifting.
  • Privilege lifting: An attacker may exploit the privilege lifting capabilities of an operating system or application to elevate privileges.
  • Password Cracking: Attackers may attempt to guess or brute force passwords to gain higher privileges.
  • Social engineering attacks: Attackers may gain privilege lifting through deception and misleading.
  • Malware: Malware may be used by attackers to gain administrator privileges.

Hazard of privilege lifting attack:

Privilege lifting attacks may lead to the following hazards:

  • Unauthorised access: An attacker may gain access to restricted resources and sensitive information.
  • Data Tampering: Attackers may tamper with data or plant malicious code.
  • System crash: A privilege lifting attack may cause system crash or service interruption.
  • Compliance violations: Elevation of privilege attacks may violate legal regulations and regulatory requirements.
  • Loss of trust: Elevation of privilege attacks can damage user and customer trust.

Methods to prevent privilege lifting attacks:

  • Regular update: update the system and applications in time, and fix known vulnerabilities.
  • Principle of Least Privilege: Give users the least necessary privileges.
  • Access Control: Correctly configure access control to limit user rights.
  • Strong Passwords: Secure accounts and systems with strong passwords and multi-factor authentication.
  • Network security: set up firewalls and intrusion detection systems to block privilege lifting attacks.
