Phishing

Introduction to phishing attacks

Phishing Attack is a common network security threat, which induces users to provide personal sensitive information, such as user names, passwords, credit card numbers, etc., by disguising themselves as credible entities or institutions. Phishing attacks are typically spread via email, text messages, social media, etc., and attempt to trick victims into believing they are communicating with a legitimate organisation.

Common types of phishing attacks:

  • Email phishing: Attackers send phishing links or attachments in emails disguised as legitimate ones, inducing victims to provide personal information or login credentials.
  • SMS Phishing: Attackers send legitimate-looking text messages asking victims to click links or respond with sensitive information.
  • Social media phishing: Attackers create fake social media pages or accounts to obtain users' personal information.
  • Phishing: Attackers use fake websites to trick users into entering sensitive information or downloading malware.

Hazards of phishing attacks:

phishing attacks can result in the following hazards:

  • Phishing attacks often aim to steal personal information, such as usernames, passwords, Social Security numbers, and credit card details. With this information, attackers can assume the victim's identity, make unauthorised transactions, and cause financial loss.
  • Phishing attacks can lead to direct financial losses when attackers gain access to banking, credit card, or other financial accounts. They can initiate unauthorised transactions, drain bank accounts, or make fraudulent purchases using stolen payment details.
  • If an attacker gains access to an individual's or organisation's system through a phishing attack, they can potentially compromise sensitive company data, customer information, and intellectual property. This can lead to data breaches with far-reaching legal, financial, and reputational consequences.

How to prevent phishing attacks:

  • Be aware of the source of emails and messages: Be cautious about opening emails and messages from unknown senders or that appear suspicious.
  • Don't click on links easily: When you receive emails or messages, don't click on links in them, especially those that ask for personal information.
  • Verify the security of the website: Make sure that the websites you visit use HTTPS encryption, and verify their legitimacy by checking the website URL.
  • Raise cybersecurity awareness: Educate employees and users to recognize the signs of a phishing attack and raise awareness about cybersecurity.
  • Use multi-factor authentication: For important accounts, use multi-factor authentication to increase account security.
