Risk Models in Cybersecurity

Risk models play a critical role in cybersecurity by helping identify and mitigate potential threats and vulnerabilities. Risk models used in cybersecurity are often designed based on specific assumptions about a system's threat, vulnerability, and consequence. Different risk models have different advantages and disadvantages.

During systems engineering, there is rarely introspection into whether a selected risk model provides the optimal insight into the posture of a system as it relates to inherent threats and vulnerabilities. Using inappropriate risk models is due to a misconception of the protection offered by different risk models. The improper use of risk models may not provide adequate protection. So, selecting the appropriate risk model for a given system design can be an essential and complex task.

Key Considerations for Risk Model Selection:

  • Understand the specific assumptions made by each risk model.
  • Evaluate the advantages and disadvantages of different risk models.
  • Ensure the risk model aligns with the system's threat and vulnerability landscape.
  • Assess the suitability of the risk model for the intended purpose.
  • Seek expert guidance to make informed risk model choices.
